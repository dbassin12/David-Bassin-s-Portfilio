The Background

I spent a decade teaching History and Economics, where I learned that the best systems don't replace human judgment — they support it. Now I help people learn what AI can actually do for them, and I build the systems to prove it.

My current setup runs five agents and 16+ automated pipelines 24/7 at near-zero cost, from job search agents that filter 85% of irrelevant roles to a voice-to-CRM pipeline deployed for a real client. That teaching background is the whole point: a 17-point scoring error caught by human override, not AI, proved that guardrails aren't optional — they're the product.

The Methodology

Three principles, applied in sequence to ensure safe AI adoption.

1
Define Guardrails First

Set scope, quality thresholds, and human review points before any language model touches your business workflow.

2
Build Deterministic Pipelines

Constrained prompts, structured JSON outputs, and rigorous audit logs produce repeatable results that teams can actually trust.

3
Iterate from Evidence

Validate outcomes, measure what changed, and ruthlessly refine—including killing automation that makes things worse. ROI is the only metric.
